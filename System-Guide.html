01

The Shopping Experience

What a customer sees, from the moment they land on the homepage to the moment their order is confirmed. No account required at any step.

Browsing & finding products

The homepage opens with a rotating image carousel (banners you control from the dashboard), followed by the full catalog. Customers narrow things down with a price range slider, category checkboxes, and a text search bar — all instant, no page reloads.

🔥 Best Seller badges

Certain products get a glowing "Best Seller Today" badge on their card. This happens one of two ways: you flag them by hand in the dashboard, or — if you switch the setting on — the system flags them automatically from real sales data. Either way, the badges stay current without you having to remember to update them.

Product pages & recommendations

Each product page shows photos, price, description, and — where you've set them up — variants: different colors or sizes of the same item, each with its own photo and stock count.

At the bottom of every product page sits a "You might also like" section, powered by AI (see Section 04). It suggests genuinely related items — a lamp alongside a nightstand, not just "same category" — and quietly falls back to a same-category-plus-popular-items method if the AI is ever unreachable. The section is never empty and never shows an error.

Out-of-stock handling

The moment a product's quantity hits zero, it becomes unavailable — it can't be added to a cart or carried through checkout. You can never accidentally oversell something you don't have.

Cart & checkout

Cart items can be added, adjusted, and removed, and the cart survives a page refresh. At checkout, the customer's delivery district drives two automatic decisions:

Business rule — payment by district

Inside Dhaka: the customer can choose either Cash on Delivery or bKash.

Outside Dhaka: Cash on Delivery isn't offered — payment must be made upfront via bKash (send-money, then a Transaction ID entered at checkout).

This exists because cash-on-delivery to distant districts carries far more loss risk for a small business, while bKash is confirmed before anything ships. If a customer switches their district from Dhaka to elsewhere mid-checkout, their payment method switches to bKash automatically — they're never left in an invalid state.

Order tracking

Two ways to check on an order: the Track Order page (order number + phone/email, no login), or My Orders for logged-in customers, showing their full history in one place.

02

Accounts & Access

A layered permission system, so different people can be trusted with different amounts of access.

RoleCan do
CustomerShop, check out, view their own orders
EmployeeManage their own product listings and fulfill their own orders
AdminFull access to orders, products, categories, customers, delivery settings
SuperadminEverything Admin can, plus sales reports, staff management, and payment settings

Employee accounts can also be given individual permissions — for example, one employee could be allowed to manage orders but not touch products or customer data. That means you can hire help without handing over the whole store.

Staff log in through a separate, private page from the customer login — keeping the two clearly apart. Any staff account can be deactivated instantly by a superadmin (e.g. an employee leaving) without deleting their account or their history.

03

The Admin Dashboard

Everything below lives behind staff login, with access shaped by the role table above.

Orders

See every order at a glance with live status. Open any order to change its status (pending → processing → shipped → delivered → cancelled), adjust the delivery charge, or add more items — using a searchable product picker with thumbnails, so staff can visually confirm they're adding the right item.

Products

Full catalog management — add, edit, delete products and their variants and photos. A "Best sellers only" filter shows exactly which products are currently flagged, whether by hand or by the automatic sales-based system.

Categories & Delivery

Categories power both the storefront filter and the AI assistant's understanding of your catalog. Delivery settings hold the two zone charges — Dhaka and Outside Dhaka — that drive the checkout pricing described in Section 01.

Users & Customers

Admins and superadmins manage staff accounts here — adding staff, editing roles and permissions, deactivating access. Every admin can browse the registered customer list and look up a customer's order history.

Reports (superadmin only)

Your sales intelligence center. Overview shows total revenue, order counts by status, average order value, and a revenue trend chart across the last 7, 30, or 90 days. Categories shows a donut chart of which product categories are earning the most, with an exact percentage breakdown and a searchable picker.

Business rule — what counts as revenue

Revenue is only counted once an order's status is set to delivered. Pending, processing, shipped, and cancelled orders are not counted — because that money hasn't actually been earned yet, or never will be. The Reports tab always reflects money you've genuinely made, not money you might make.

One nuance: the day/week/month totals bucket by when the order was placed, not when it was marked delivered — so an order placed two weeks ago and delivered today counts toward all-time revenue, but not "today's" revenue.

04

The AI Features

Three features, all built on Google's Gemini AI, sharing one important limit.

Shared limit — daily quota

The free-tier API key allows roughly 20 AI requests per day, total, across all three features combined. When that's hit, every feature is designed to fail gracefully — customers never see an error, they just get a slightly simpler experience until the quota resets the next day.

🤖 Shopping chat assistant

A chat bubble on every page. Customers can ask things in plain language — English or Bangla, and the assistant replies in whichever language they used. It's instructed to never invent a product, price, or stock number; it's required to actually search the real, live catalog before answering anything. To stretch the daily quota further, it remembers answers to common opening questions for six hours and reuses them instantly instead of asking the AI again.

🔍 Smart product search

When a search turns up no results — a vague query like "something cheap for gifting" — a "✨ Try AI search" button appears. Clicking it sends the query to the AI, which turns loose, conversational language into structured filters (keyword, category, price range) and re-searches. If the AI can't be reached, the site quietly falls back to a plain keyword search using the customer's own words — search never simply breaks.

💡 "You might also like"

Shown on every product page, suggesting genuinely related items rather than just same-category ones. Recommendations are cached for 24 hours per product to save on quota, and fall back to same-category-plus-popular-items if the AI is unavailable.

05

Design & Polish

Smaller details that make the site feel considered rather than assembled.

  • Branded loading indicators everywhere data is being fetched, instead of a blank or frozen screen
  • A consistent visual identity — the same warm terracotta-and-cream palette across the storefront and the dashboard
  • Colorblind-safe sales charts, consistent between the Overview and Categories report tabs
  • Mobile-friendly filters — search and the price slider stay usable even on small screens
